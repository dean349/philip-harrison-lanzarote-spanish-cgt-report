How Spanish Capital Gains Tax Works

When a non-resident sells Spanish property, the government charges IRNR (Impuesto sobre la Renta de No Residentes) — Spain’s CGT for foreign owners.

Step 1 — Completion Day (Modelo 211)

The buyer’s solicitor withholds 3% of the sale price automatically and pays it to AEAT within 30 days.

ⓘ Key FactOn a €320,000 sale: €9,600 is withheld automatically on completion day.

Step 2 — Within 3 Months (Modelo 210)

Philip files a Modelo 210 return showing all deductions. Final tax rate: 19%.

OutcomeWhat Happens
Final tax < €9,600Spain refunds the difference (6–18 months)
Final tax > €9,600Philip pays the balance within 3 months
